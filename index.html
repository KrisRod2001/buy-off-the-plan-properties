What is Off-the-Plan Buying?

Off-the-plan purchases involve buying a property before construction is complete, often based on plans and artist impressions. Popular in growing areas of Melbourne, Brisbane, and regional centres, these properties offer modern designs and potential capital growth.

Specialised conveyancing is essential due to the unique contracts and longer timelines involved.

Key Benefits of Off-the-Plan Properties

  • Lower entry price compared to completed properties
  • Potential for capital growth by settlement date
  • Brand new fixtures, fittings, and warranties
  • Stamp duty savings (often calculated on contract price, not final value)
  • First home buyer grants and concessions frequently apply
  • Customisation options on finishes and layouts
  • Depreciation benefits for investors

Understanding Risks and How to Protect Yourself

While rewarding, off-the-plan buys carry risks:

  • Developer insolvency or project delays
  • Final property differing from plans
  • Market value changes by settlement
  • Sunset clause misuse by developers

A skilled conveyancer reviews the contract thoroughly, negotiates protective clauses, and ensures compliance with state laws.

The Off-the-Plan Conveyancing Process

  • Detailed contract and plan review before signing
  • Checks on developer track record and financing
  • Negotiation of sunset dates and variation clauses
  • Ongoing monitoring during construction
  • Pre-settlement inspection of the completed property
  • Final adjustments and electronic settlement
